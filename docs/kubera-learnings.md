### 3. Catalyst-driven vs systematic alpha — the *setup vs catalyst* distinction

This is the single most important methodology insight in the document. **The model does not predict catalysts.** It predicts *setups* — combinations of factors (oversold + earnings reaction + volume confirmation + MA cross + sector rotation signal) that have historically had elevated 5-day forward returns. When a catalyst then lands during the 5-day holding window, the upside-tail is captured.

Worked example: **TTD March 5, 2026 (+11.86% single-day excess — program record)**
- Setup identification: model's classifier picked TTD as top-5 long for **4 consecutive trading days** (Feb 27 / Mar 2 / Mar 3 / Mar 4) on what appears to be post-Q4-earnings momentum + oversold mean-reversion + volume confirmation
- Catalyst alignment: Mar 5 brought a single-day +18.36% TTD move on 8× normal volume (likely a coordinated short squeeze + specific positive news)
- Exit discipline: the Feb 27 lot was sold Mar 5 at $31.49 (entry $23.08) for +36.4% in 5 trading days. **Without the 5-day holding rule, the gain would have evaporated by April** (TTD bled back to $21 by month-end).

### 5. Tax drag asymmetry — the structural reason 2022/2018/2025 dominate

Standard short-term capital gains drag in the dashboard is 30% (US STCG). This creates a deeply asymmetric profile:

**Bull years (strategy positive, SPY positive):**
- Strategy +X% gross
- Strategy +X × 0.70 = +0.70X% after-tax
- SPY +Y% (after-LTCG ~+Y × 0.85)
- **A/T excess = 0.70X - Y** ⟹ strategy must beat SPY by **at least Y/0.70 = +43% of SPY's gain** just to break even after-tax

**Bear years (strategy positive, SPY negative):**
- Strategy +X% gross → 0.70X% after-tax
- SPY -Y% (no tax on loss)
- **A/T excess = 0.70X + Y** — both effects add together

This is why the top-3 excess years are:
1. 2025 +55.50% (strategy +73% a/t in a SPY +18% year — exceptional gross alpha overcame the tax drag)
2. 2022 +37.12% (strategy +18.5% a/t in a SPY -18.7% year — both effects added)
3. 2018 +35.35% (strategy +30.1% a/t in a SPY -5.3% year — same mechanism)

**Methodology implication:** for tax-deferred accounts (IRA, Roth, 401k), the dashboard a/t numbers UNDERSTATE realistic returns substantially. The strategy is structurally well-suited for tax-deferred wrappers, where the 30% drag disappears. Live deployment in a taxable account should accept that ~30% of gross gain is paid annually to the IRS.

### 6. Year-boundary tax-reset artifact

A subtlety in the dashboard: the a/t cumulative multiple drops every January 1 by 14-32% of the *prior year's* gross gain. This is **not a strategy loss** — it's the prior-year STCG bill becoming "realized" in the after-tax accounting line.

Examples:
- Jan 2023: a/t cum dropped 9.96× → 9.59× (-3.7% on a small 2022 gain)
- Jan 2024: a/t cum dropped 11.74× → 9.96× (-15% on the 2023 +58% gross)
- Jan 2026: a/t cum dropped 46.55× → 38.90× (-16% on the 2025 +105% gross)

**Read the a/t cum line as a "saw-tooth" curve with annual resets, NOT as a clean exponential.** The pre-tax cum (dotted line on the equity chart) is the true compounding picture.

### 7. The Mag-7 structural underweight — the model's biggest blind spot

Reviewing the 148 months of holdings data, the model picked top-5 mega-cap-tech names (NVDA, MSFT, META, AAPL, GOOG, AMZN) for **less than 15% of trading days across the entire program**. NVDA appeared in Aug 2023 (winning +9.55%) and a few other moments, but MSFT did not appear in any top-10 holdings list until Jun 2025 (1.3% allocation).

The structural reason: the model's training data is per-stock predictions across the SP500 universe, weighted equally. Mid-cap names with idiosyncratic catalyst potential generate stronger classifier signals than mega-caps that mostly track the index. This means:

**Cost:** every Mag-7-led narrow rally (Jan 2024 -2.92, Feb 2024 -1.29, May 2024 -3.60, Jan 2026 -2.54) is a structural miss.
**Benefit:** the model captures mid-cap idiosyncratic alpha (Sep 2025 ON Semi, Mar 2026 TTD, Aug 2022 NVDA exception) that mega-cap-only strategies cannot.

**Recommendation: do NOT try to "patch in" Mag-7 exposure.** It would dilute the idiosyncratic-catalyst signal that drives the program-record excess months. Accept the up-tape misses as the cost of the structural alpha.

### 8. Volatility regime sensitivity

The strategy's excess scales non-linearly with the volatility regime:

| VIX peak in year | Mean a/t excess across 12 years |
|---|---|
| < 20 (2017 Goldilocks) | +8.7% |
| 20-35 (2014, 2019, 2021, 2023, 2024) | +9.6% |
| 35-50 (2018, 2022, 2024, 2026 partial) | +21.5% |
| 50-65 (2015, 2024 Yen-carry, 2025 Liberation Day) | +25.6% |
| > 65 (2020 COVID) | +18.4% |

**The sweet spot is VIX 35-65.** In ultra-low-vol regimes (2017), the model's relative-value signals are muted because dispersion is low. In extreme-vol regimes (2020 COVID), the signal-to-noise ratio degrades because catalyst-driven moves get overwhelmed by macro flow.

**Methodology implication:** the strategy will likely **underperform its 12-year average in any future low-VIX environment** (a return to 2017-style Goldilocks). Plan for ~+5-8% a/t excess years rather than the +20+% the 2018/2022/2025 years deliver.

**May 2026 confirmation:** VIX averaged ~16 in May 2026 (low-vol bull tape) and the strategy delivered −4.39% alpha — its worst OOS month and the cleanest example yet of the documented sweet-spot failure mode. The stop-out rate jumped from program-average ~30% to 55%, the fingerprint of "bounces aborted before reverting because the underlying signal is genuinely weaker." No action item — this is within-distribution behavior, not a regime break, and the +14.93% YTD alpha through May confirms the structural edge survives.

### 10. Concrete methodology recommendations for going forward

1. **Don't reduce concentration limits.** The 67% hit rate × 4-6:1 asymmetric payoff means 25-50% single-name allocations are net-positive expected value. Capping position size below 30% would gut the upside-tail mechanism.

2. **Don't extend the 5-day holding window.** TTD Mar 2026 chart proved the catalyst-driven gains decay within 7-10 days. The 5-day exit discipline is a load-bearing piece of the alpha.

3. **Don't try to add mega-cap-tech exposure to fix the Mag-7-narrow-rally misses.** It would dilute the idiosyncratic signal that drives the +10% excess months.

4. **Do surface the "consecutive-day-same-pick" signal as a separate dashboard metric.** When the classifier picks the same name as top-1 for 3+ consecutive days, the catalyst hit rate is ~70%. This is the most actionable signal in the model and currently buried inside the holdings aggregation.

5. **Do deploy in tax-deferred wrappers preferentially.** The 30% STCG drag is real and structural. Strategy is ideal for IRA / Roth / 401k where the drag disappears.

6. **Do plan for ~+10-25 pts a/t excess per year as the realistic forward expectation,** not the +55 pt 2025 outlier. 2025 was the confluence of multiple favorable regimes (rate-cut + tariff vol + AI broadening). Normal years should be the 2014 / 2019 / 2023 range.

7. **Do investigate why bull cases got smashed 6 times.** Specifically: the months Aug 2022, Sep 2025, Oct 2025, Nov 2024, Nov 2023, Mar 2026 all delivered 2-6× the bull-case ceiling. These were not lucky — they were *systematic* catalyst-alignment events. The outlook framework should be retrained to recognize the precursors (4-day-consecutive-top-pick + VIX 30+ + sector dispersion >= 80th percentile).

8. **Do extend the backtest periodically.** The 2024 → 2026 extension produced more methodology insight than any prior period. Re-running with a fresh 6-12 months of OOS data every year is cheap (1 day of compute) and validates that the structural alpha hasn't decayed.

9. **Do NOT promise 50%+ annualized returns to anyone in marketing materials.** The 12-year-4-month program annualized 49.93% pre-tax / 34.91% a/t — but this includes the 2025 +105% gross outlier. Stripped of 2025, the program annualizes closer to 38% pre-tax / 27% a/t — still extraordinary, more defensible, and less likely to disappoint live.

10. **Do read the failures harder than the wins.** The 5 worst miss-months (Apr 2024 PSKY no-catalyst, Oct 2023 KEY+cruises, May 2024 SMCI miss, Nov 2022 cyclical-drag, Jan 2026 Mag-7 miss) all share a structural feature: **the model held a concentrated bet that did NOT have a catalyst land in the 5-day window.** That's the failure mode — and it's the price paid for the wins. Understanding it deeply is the difference between disciplined deployment and panic-exiting after a -3% month.
